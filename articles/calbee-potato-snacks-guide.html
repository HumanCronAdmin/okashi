how many things can you do with a potato?

calbee asked that question and then spent decades answering it. sticks in a cup. thick-cut chips that fight back when you bite them. shrimp crackers that aren't even potato but somehow feel like they belong. whole-cut fries in a box. all from the same company, all completely different textures, all in every konbini in the country for under ¥200.

i go through calbee stuff faster than anything else in my pantry. there's always a jagariko cup on my desk and a bag of kappa ebisen somewhere near the couch. they're just part of the furniture at this point.

the lineup

jagariko salad flavor

this is the og for a lot of people, myself included. it's those little crunchy potato sticks in a cup. the salad flavor has these tiny flecks of parsley and carrot, which sounds weird for a chip, but it just adds this subtle freshness. it's salty and herb-y, and the crunch is so satisfying. i always grab a cup when i'm waiting for the train or just need something to do with my hands while i'm on my laptop.

the texture is super consistent, like a really dry, crispy french fry. it's not greasy at all, which is a big plus. sometimes i'll mix it with a little bit of cheese powder if i'm feeling fancy, but it's perfect as is. the cup makes it easy to eat anywhere, no mess.

jagariko cheese

if you're a cheese lover, this is your jam. it's got that same satisfying crunch as the salad flavor, but the cheese flavor is way more prominent. it's a rich, savory cheese taste that just hits the spot. i find myself reaching for this one more in the evenings, maybe while i'm watching something on tv. it feels a bit more indulgent than the salad.

it's not an overpowering cheese, though. it's more of a creamy, savory profile that doesn't leave a weird aftertaste. the sticks themselves are sturdy, so you don't get a lot of broken bits at the bottom, which is nice. a solid choice when you want something cheesy and crunchy.

calbee kata-age chips lightly salted

kata-age means "hard-fried," and these chips live up to the name. they are seriously crunchy, like, you can hear the crunch from across the room. they're cut thicker than your average chip, and that gives them this really substantial bite. the lightly salted flavor is super simple, just pure potato and salt.

i love these when i'm just sitting at my desk and need a little pick-me-up. the salt hits your tongue first, and then you get that deep potato flavor. they're not flimsy at all, so you don't have to worry about them breaking in the bag. it's a really honest, straightforward potato chip experience.

calbee kappa ebisen

these are a classic, been around forever. they're these light, airy, crispy little shrimp-flavored snacks. they smell faintly of the sea when you open the bag, which is kind of unique. the flavor is subtle, not fishy at all, more like a savory, slightly salty umami. they're so delicate, they almost melt in your mouth.

i usually buy these when i'm out and about and want something light to munch on. they're really easy to share, and people who haven't tried them are always surprised by the flavor. it's one of those snacks where the bag is always empty before you realize it. "you can't stop eating them" is pretty accurate.

jagabee lightly salted

jagabee are like the cousins of jagariko, but they're made from whole potato sticks, not mashed and extruded. they have a slightly more natural potato taste and a different kind of crunch – a bit more hollow, maybe? the lightly salted flavor is clean and simple. i like these when i want something that feels a bit more like actual potato.

they come in a cup too, which is convenient. i often find myself eating these on the balcony when it's a nice day, just enjoying the simple flavor and the crisp texture. they're less intensely flavored than jagariko, which can be good if you want something milder.

which one first?

if you like a strong, savory flavor and a really loud crunch, go for jagariko cheese. if you prefer something lighter and a bit herby, the jagariko salad is your best bet. for a no-nonsense, thick potato chip, kata-age is the way to go.

where to find them

you can find all of these at pretty much any konbini (7-eleven, family mart, lawson), supermarkets, and even drugstores across japan. they're also usually available on amazon japan if you're looking to stock up.
