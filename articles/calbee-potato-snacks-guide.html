my daily potato stick ritual

i get asked all the time what snacks to buy in japan, especially when people are talking about konbini finds. and sure, the pocky and matcha kit kats are fine, they're the gateway drugs. but once you've had those, there's a whole world of potato snacks waiting. calbee is the king of this world, and i spend way too much money on their stuff every week. it started because i got tired of explaining the same five things to my friends, so i built this whole database. now i can just point them to it. but for you guys, i'll break down some of the essentials, the ones i always have in my pantry.

the lineup

jagariko salad flavor (じゃがりこ サラダ)

these are probably the most iconic. you see the little cartoon eyes everywhere. the salad flavor is the original, and it's got this subtle salty, herby taste. imagine a really crisp french fry, but in stick form. they're super dry and crunchy, perfect for when you need a loud snack. the little bits of parsley and carrot feel fancy, even though they’re probably just for show.

the texture is key here. it’s a satisfying snap with every bite. i eat these mostly when i’m bored, like waiting for a train or just scrolling through my phone at my desk. they’re not too filling, which is good and bad.

jagariko cheese (じゃがりこ チーズ)

if you like cheese, you have to try these. they’re like the salad flavor but with a much richer, more intense cheese powder coating. it’s that classic japanese cheese flavor, not too sharp, not too mild. still super crunchy, of course.

these are a bit more decadent than the salad ones. i find myself reaching for these when i want something a bit more satisfying, maybe in the evening while watching something. they’re a bit more of a treat.

calbee kata-age chips lightly salted (カルビー 堅あげポテト うすしお味)

kata-age means "hard fried," and they really mean it. these chips are thick, almost like little potato planks. the crunch is serious – it’s a deep, satisfying sound. the lightly salted flavor is super clean. you taste the potato first, then the salt hits.

these are the opposite of flimsy chips. they’re substantial. i like these when i want something that feels a bit more "real" potato. good for a mid-afternoon pick-me-up, or when you just need a solid, no-nonsense potato crunch.

calbee kappa ebisen (カルビー かっぱえびせん)

these have been around forever, and for good reason. they’re incredibly light and airy, almost like they’d float away. the shrimp flavor is subtle but there, a savory, slightly salty taste. they’re made with real shrimp, which you can actually taste.

the saying with these is "you can't stop eating them," and it’s true. they’re so easy to snack on. the texture is delicate and crispy, not hard like jagariko. perfect for mindless munching while you’re doing something else.

jagabee lightly salted (jagabee うすしお味)

jagabee are like a more rustic version of jagariko. they’re made from whole potato pieces, so they have a slightly more natural, less processed potato flavor. they're also really crunchy, but maybe a touch less uniformly so than jagariko.

the lightly salted ones are my go-to for a simple, clean potato flavor. they’re great for when you want that potato satisfaction without any strong seasonings. i often grab these for lunch breaks or when i’m on the go.

quick comparison

jagariko cheese super crunchy sticks 2 evening treat ~160 yen
kata-age salted thick, hard crunch 1 mid-afternoon boost ~150 yen
kappa ebisen light, airy crisp 1 mindless munching ~130 yen
jagabee salted crunchy, whole potato 1 lunch break, on-the-go ~160 yen

which one first?

if you want the classic japanese snack experience, go for jagariko salad. if you prefer a more substantial, no-frills potato crunch, kata-age chips are your best bet. and for something light and addictive, you can't beat kappa ebisen.

where to find them

you can find these calbee snacks pretty much everywhere. konbini like 7-eleven, family mart, and lawson will have a good selection, though prices are a bit higher. supermarkets are your best bet for variety and better prices, especially if you buy multipacks. they're also easily available on amazon japan if you're ordering from overseas.
