pocky is just the beginning

everybody knows pocky. it’s the thing people buy at the airport on the way home. but glico — the company behind pocky — makes stuff that never leaves japan and probably should. pretz is the savory older brother that nobody talks about. cheeza is a cracker that’s more cheese than cracker. same company, completely different vibes.

what i like about glico is the range. sweet, salty, cheesy — they cover all the moods. and everything just works. you grab a box without thinking and it’s always good. that’s hard to pull off across that many different snacks.

the lineup

pocky chocolate (ポッキー チョコレート)

hold the plain end, bite the chocolate end. that crunch is so specific — thin pretzel snapping through a coat of milk chocolate. not thick, not fancy, just right. i bring a box when i go to someone’s house. everyone reaches in without asking. it’s one of those snacks that erases awkwardness.

here’s something most people don’t know: pocky was born in 1966, three years after pretz. glico made the savory stick first, then dipped it in chocolate. the little brother became more famous than the big brother.

pocky matcha green tea (ポッキー 抹茶)

open the box and you smell it before you taste it — earthy, green, a little bitter. the coating is thinner than regular pocky, so the pretzel crunch comes through more. it pairs with unsweetened green tea in a way that feels intentional. i had these with my japanese teacher once during a lesson and she said "ah, that’s a good combination." felt like i passed a test.

pocky strawberry (ポッキー つぶつぶいちご)

the pink one with tiny strawberry bits in the coating — tsubu-tsubu, they call it. you can feel the little bumps on your tongue. it’s sweeter than the other flavors, more candy-like. i split a box with friends on the shinkansen once and everyone fought over the last three sticks. that’s the kind of snack this is.

pretz salad (プリッツ サラダ)

no chocolate, no sweetness. just a seasoned pretzel stick with herbs and salt. the crunch is louder than pocky because there’s nothing to soften it. the salt hits your tongue first, then the herbs come through. i eat these when i want savory but don’t want to commit to a whole bag of chips. nobody ever talks about pretz, which is a shame because it’s been around longer than pocky.

cheeza cheddar cheese (チーザ チェダーチーズ)

this is glico’s weirdest product and maybe their best. thin crispy chips that are over 50% real cheese. snap one in half and look at the cross-section — it’s dense. the cheese smell when you open the bag is strong. glico designed these as a drinking snack, not a regular snack. pair it with wine or beer and it makes sense. my coworker brought a bag to a friday night hangout and it was gone in four minutes.

quick comparison

pocky matcha crispy, slightly bitter coating 2 afternoon tea ¥180
pocky strawberry crispy, crunchy bits 4 sharing with friends ¥180
pretz salad very crispy, light 1 train station snack ¥120
cheeza cheddar cheese thin, crispy, melts 1 evening relaxation ¥200

which one first?

if you’re new to glico and want the classic experience, go for pocky chocolate. if you prefer savory, pretz salad is the way to go. and if you’re a serious cheese lover, you have to try cheeza.

where to find them

you can find all of these at pretty much any konbini (7-eleven, family mart, lawson) or any supermarket across japan. they’re also readily available on sites like amazon japan if you’re outside the country.
