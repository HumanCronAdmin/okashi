the original crunch that started it all

glico. it’s one of those names you just see everywhere when you live in japan. they’re behind so many of the snacks i’ve been eating since i was a kid. and when my friends from overseas ask me what to buy here, i always end up talking about glico first. it’s not just about pocky, though that’s usually where people start. they’ve got this whole range of stuff that’s just… dependable. it’s the kind of snack you can grab without thinking and know it’s going to be good.

i’ve built this whole database because answering the same questions gets tiring, lol. but glico’s lineup is so solid, it’s worth talking about. they’ve got the classics that everyone knows, but also some hidden gems that are just as good, if not better. it’s the small joys, you know? like finding that perfect balance of salty and crispy, or that moment the chocolate just melts in your mouth.

the lineup

pocky chocolate (ポッキー チョコレート)

okay, the og. the one everyone knows. thin, crispy pretzel stick dipped in just the right amount of milk chocolate. it’s not super fancy, but that’s the point. the chocolate coating is just thick enough to give you that satisfying snap when you bite into it, but not so thick that it’s messy. i usually grab a box when i’m heading to a friend’s place or just need something to munch on while i’m working late at my desk.

the best part is probably the sound it makes. that little *crunch* is so satisfying. and if you’re lucky, you might even find a box with a few extra chocolate-dipped ends. it’s the little things, right?

pocky matcha green tea (ポッキー 抹茶)

this is where things get a little more refined. the matcha pocky has this lovely aromatic green tea flavor that’s not too sweet. it’s got a slight bitterness, which i actually prefer sometimes over the regular chocolate. it feels a bit more grown-up, if that makes sense.

i love eating these when i’m having a quiet afternoon tea. it pairs perfectly with a cup of unsweetened green tea. the color is also really pretty, a nice muted green that looks good on a plate.

pocky strawberry (ポッキー つぶつぶいちご)

if you like sweet and fruity, this is your jam. the strawberry cream coating is bright pink and has these tiny little bits of dried strawberry mixed in. they call it ‘tsubu-tsubu,’ which means bumpy or grainy, and it’s exactly that. it adds a nice little textural surprise.

these are perfect for sharing, especially with people who aren’t huge chocolate fans. i remember splitting a box with some friends on the shinkansen once, and everyone was fighting over the last few sticks. they’re just fun.

pretz salad (プリッツ サラダ)

now, if you’re not in the mood for chocolate, there’s always pretz. the salad flavor is my go-to. it’s basically a plain, savory pretzel stick seasoned with a light herb blend. it’s salty, a little herby, and super addictive.

these are great for when you want something savory but not too heavy. i often have a pack when i’m waiting for the train or just need a little something to tide me over before dinner. the crunch is really satisfying, and the saltiness hits your tongue first.

cheeza cheddar cheese (チーザ チェダーチーズ)

cheeza is something else entirely. these are thin, crispy chips that are packed with real cheddar cheese. like, they say it’s over 50% real cheese. you can really taste it. it’s intense, savory, and has this melt-in-your-mouth quality.

i usually have these when i’m relaxing at home, maybe watching something on my phone at night. the flavor is so concentrated, you only need a few to feel satisfied. they can be a bit crumbly, but the taste is totally worth it.

quick comparison

pocky chocolate crispy, smooth chocolate 3 3pm desk snack ¥150
pocky matcha crispy, slightly bitter coating 2 afternoon tea ¥180
pocky strawberry crispy, crunchy bits 4 sharing with friends ¥180
pretz salad very crispy, light 1 train station snack ¥120
cheeza cheddar cheese thin, crispy, melts 1 evening relaxation ¥200

which one first?

if you’re new to glico and want the classic experience, go for pocky chocolate. if you prefer savory, pretz salad is the way to go. and if you’re a serious cheese lover, you have to try cheeza.

where to find them

you can find all of these at pretty much any konbini (7-eleven, family mart, lawson) or any supermarket across japan. they’re also readily available on sites like amazon japan if you’re outside the country.
