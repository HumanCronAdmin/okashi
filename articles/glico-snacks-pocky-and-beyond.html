pocky pretz cheeza: glico's hits beyond the basic

i get asked all the time by friends visiting japan, "what should i actually buy at a konbini?" and it gets tiring saying the same things. like yeah, kitkat matcha is good, and pocky chocolate is… pocky. but there's so much more. i built this whole database because i was sick of it. glico is one of those brands that has a few really famous things, but they also have some stuff that’s just as good, if not better, and people miss out. this is for them.

the lineup

pocky chocolate (ポッキー チョコレート)

this is the one everyone knows. thin, crispy pretzel sticks dipped in a decent milk chocolate. it’s not super fancy chocolate, but that’s kind of the point. it’s reliable. the crunch is satisfying, and it’s just sweet enough for that 3pm desk slump or when you’re waiting for the train. you can find it everywhere.

pocky matcha green tea (ポッキー 抹茶)

okay, this one is a step up from the basic chocolate. the matcha flavor is pretty authentic, not too sweet, with that slight earthy bitterness. it’s more refined. the chocolate coating is a bit thinner than the original, which i actually prefer. it feels a bit more grown-up, you know? good with a cup of unsweetened green tea.

pocky strawberry (ポッキー つぶつぶいちご)

this one has little bits of real strawberry in the coating, which is a nice touch. it’s noticeably sweeter than the other pocky flavors. the strawberry flavor is pretty strong, almost like a strawberry candy. it’s fun and a bit nostalgic, like something you’d eat as a kid, but the bits add a little something extra.

pretz salad (プリッツ サラダ)

this is where we leave the sweet stuff behind. pretz are just the pretzel sticks without any coating. the salad flavor is the classic. it’s savory, with a light herb seasoning. the crunch is really prominent here because there’s no chocolate to soften it. it’s super addictive, especially if you like salty snacks. i find myself eating a whole box way too quickly.

cheeza cheddar cheese (チーザ チェダーチーズ)

cheeza is different. these are thin, almost cracker-like chips, and they are packed with real cheese. like, over 50% cheese. the cheddar flavor is intense and savory. it’s not sweet at all. the texture is super crispy, almost brittle. it’s a snack for when you want something really cheesy and satisfying. a bit more expensive, but worth it.

quick comparison

snack texture sweetness (1-5) best moment konbini price (approx.)
pocky chocolate crispy pretzel, smooth coating 3 anytime ¥150
pocky matcha crispy pretzel, slightly bitter coating 2 afternoon break ¥180
pocky strawberry crispy pretzel, sweet coating with bits 4 dessert ¥180
pretz salad very crispy, savory 1 with a drink ¥150
cheeza cheddar thin, brittle crisp 1 late night ¥200

which one first?

if you want the classic japanese snack experience, go for pocky chocolate or matcha. if you’re more into savory and want something super crunchy, pretz salad is your go-to. and if you’re a serious cheese lover who wants an intense flavor hit, cheeza is the one.

where to find them

you can find all of these at pretty much any konbini (7-eleven, family mart, lawson) or any supermarket across japan. larger ones might have more variety. they are also usually available on japanese online shopping sites like amazon. more about glico: glico brand page.
