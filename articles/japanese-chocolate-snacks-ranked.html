The Ultimate Guide to Japanese Chocolate Snacks: A Connoisseur's Rankings

As a long-time resident of Japan and a self-proclaimed chocolate enthusiast, I've had the pleasure of sampling an array of delectable Japanese chocolate snacks. However, with the sheer variety of options available, choosing the perfect treat can be a daunting task. Foreigners living in Japan, tourists planning trips, and overseas fans ordering Japanese snacks online often struggle to decide which chocolate snack to indulge in. This guide aims to provide you with an insider's perspective on the best Japanese chocolate snacks, helping you make an informed decision.

The Best Picks

Pocky Chocolate

Pocky Chocolate by Glico is a classic Japanese snack that needs no introduction. These thin pretzel sticks dipped in milk chocolate are the original and best-selling flavor worldwide. The perfect combination of crunchy and smooth textures will leave you wanting more. Enjoy Pocky Chocolate on its own or paired with a steaming cup of coffee for the ultimate Japanese experience.

I recommend trying the original flavor first, as it's the most authentic representation of Pocky Chocolate. You can find Pocky Chocolate at most convenience stores and supermarkets in Japan, as well as online retailers.

Koala's March Chocolate

Koala's March Chocolate by Lotte is a delightful treat that's sure to put a smile on your face. Bite-sized koala-shaped biscuits filled with chocolate cream make for a delightful snack. Each koala has a unique print, adding to the charm of this Japanese classic. Koala's March Chocolate is perfect for sharing with friends or enjoying on your own.

Be sure to try the original flavor, as it's the most popular and widely available. You can find Koala's March Chocolate at convenience stores, supermarkets, and online retailers in Japan.

Takenoko no Sato

Takenoko no Sato by Meiji is a popular snack that's sure to delight chocolate lovers. Bamboo shoot-shaped cookies topped with chocolate make for a unique and delicious treat. While some may prefer the rival Kinoko no Yama, Takenoko no Sato holds its own in the great snack debate. Give it a try and decide for yourself!

Try the original flavor first, as it's the most authentic representation of Takenoko no Sato. You can find this snack at convenience stores, supermarkets, and online retailers in Japan.

Meiji Milk Chocolate

Meiji Milk Chocolate is a classic Japanese snack that's been a favorite since 1926. Smooth, creamy, and perfectly balanced, this milk chocolate bar is a must-try for any chocolate enthusiast. Enjoy Meiji Milk Chocolate on its own or paired with a cup of coffee for a delightful treat.

Be sure to try the original flavor, as it's the most authentic representation of Meiji Milk Chocolate. You can find this snack at convenience stores, supermarkets, and online retailers in Japan.

Toppo Chocolate

Toppo Chocolate by Lotte is a unique snack that's sure to intrigue. Pretzel tubes filled with chocolate inside make for a delightful treat. The chocolate won't melt on your hands, making it perfect for snacking on the go. Give Toppo Chocolate a try and experience the inverse of Pocky.

Try the original flavor first, as it's the most popular and widely available. You can find Toppo Chocolate at convenience stores, supermarkets, and online retailers in Japan.

Alfort Mini Chocolate

Rich chocolate layered on a whole wheat biscuit with a sailing ship embossed design makes for a delightful snack. Give Alfort Mini Chocolate a try and experience the perfect combination of flavors and textures.

Try the original flavor, as it's the most authentic representation of Alfort Mini Chocolate. You can find this snack at convenience stores, supermarkets, and online retailers in Japan.

Black Thunder

Black Thunder by Yuraku Confectionery is a dense chocolate bar packed with cocoa cookie and biscuit crunch. Huge flavor for its tiny price makes Black Thunder a must-try for any chocolate enthusiast. Give it a try and experience the perfect combination of textures and flavors.

Try the original flavor first, as it's the most popular and widely available. You can find Black Thunder at convenience stores, supermarkets, and online retailers in Japan.

Royce' Nama Chocolate

Royce' Nama Chocolate is a luxurious treat that's sure to impress any chocolate connoisseur. Ultra-smooth ganache squares dusted with cocoa powder make for a delightful snack. Made in Hokkaido with fresh cream, Royce' Nama Chocolate is a must-try for anyone looking to indulge in high-quality chocolate.

Be sure to try the original flavor, as it's the most authentic representation of Royce' Nama Chocolate. You can find this snack at high-end retailers and online stores in Japan.

Meiji Apollo Strawberry Chocolate

Meiji Apollo Strawberry Chocolate is a unique treat that's sure to delight. Cone-shaped chocolate with strawberry and milk chocolate layers make for a delightful snack. Named after the Apollo spacecraft, this chocolate is perfect for sharing with friends or enjoying on your own.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't assume that all Japanese chocolate snacks are the same. Each has its unique flavor and texture, so be sure to try them individually.
  • Avoid buying limited edition snacks, as they often disappear quickly and may not be worth the hype.
  • Don't confuse Japanese chocolate snacks with Western-style chocolate bars. While they may look similar, the flavors and textures are often distinct.

Pro Tips from Japan

  • Try purchasing Japanese chocolate snacks at konbini (convenience stores) instead of supermarkets, as prices tend to be lower.
  • Be sure to check the expiration dates of your snacks, as some may have limited shelf life.
  • Consider purchasing Japanese chocolate snacks at seasonal markets or festivals for rare and unique flavors.
