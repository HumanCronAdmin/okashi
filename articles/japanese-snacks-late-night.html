The Perfect Japanese Snack for Your Late-Night Cravings

Japanese snacks are famous worldwide for their unique flavors and addictive textures, but with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the perfect one for your late-night cravings. With the rising popularity of Japanese snacks abroad, you might be wondering which ones to try or which ones to gift to friends who love Japan.

As a food writer living in Japan, I've tried countless Japanese snacks and have a deep understanding of their flavor profiles, textures, and best ways to enjoy them. In this article, I'll introduce you to my top picks for Japanese snacks that are perfect for late-night munching.

The Best Picks

Jagariko Salad Flavor (じゃがりこ サラダ)

Jagariko Salad Flavor by Calbee is a cup of crunchy potato sticks with parsley and carrot bits. The addictive crunch is a perfect combination of salty and herb flavors. It's a great snack to munch on while watching your favorite TV show or working on a project.

Texture: Crunchy Flavors: Salty, Herb Best way to enjoy: Straight from the cup Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Jagariko Cheese (じゃがりこ チーズ)

Jagariko Cheese by Calbee is another popular snack from the same brand. Crunchy potato sticks with rich cheese flavor make it a favorite among Japanese snackers. It's a great snack to enjoy with a hot cup of coffee or tea.

Texture: Crunchy Flavors: Cheese Best way to enjoy: Straight from the cup Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Calbee Kata-Age Chips Lightly Salted (カルビー 堅あげポテト うすしお味)

Calbee Kata-Age Chips Lightly Salted by Calbee is a thick-cut, hard-fried potato chip with a satisfying crunch. Simple salt flavor makes it a great snack for those who prefer a light taste. It's perfect for munching on while reading a book or browsing your phone.

Texture: Crunchy Flavors: Salty Best way to enjoy: Straight from the bag Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Kaki no Tane (柿の種)

Kaki no Tane by Kameda Seika is a crescent-shaped spicy rice cracker mixed with peanuts. Japan's #1 beer snack since 1966, it's a classic that's perfect for those who like a little spice in their snacks. It's great to enjoy with a cold beer or a glass of wine.

Texture: Crunchy Flavors: Salty, Spicy Best way to enjoy: Straight from the bag Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Cheeza Cheddar Cheese (チーザ チェダーチーズ)

Cheeza Cheddar Cheese by Glico is over 50% real cheese content, making it a cheese lover's dream come true. Intense, concentrated cheese flavor in thin crispy chips makes it a great snack for those who love cheese. It's perfect for enjoying with a cup of coffee or tea.

Texture: Crispy Flavors: Cheese Best way to enjoy: Straight from the cup Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Kappa Ebisen 100% Sakura Shrimp (かっぱえびせん 桜えび100%使用)

Kappa Ebisen 100% Sakura Shrimp by Calbee is a premium version using 100% sakura shrimp from Suruga Bay. Deeper, more refined shrimp flavor makes it a great snack for those who love seafood. It's perfect for enjoying with a cold beer or a glass of wine.

Texture: Crispy Flavors: Shrimp, Salty Best way to enjoy: Straight from the cup Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Bakauke Sesame Fried Soy Sauce (ばかうけ ごま揚醤油)

Bakauke Sesame Fried Soy Sauce by Kuriyama Beika is deep-fried rice crackers with roasted sesame and soy sauce glaze. Rich, savory, and crunchy, it's a great snack for those who love umami flavors. It's perfect for enjoying with a cup of coffee or tea.

Texture: Crunchy Flavors: Soy-sauce, Sesame Best way to enjoy: Straight from the bag Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Don Tacos Beef Chili Tacos (どんタコス ビーフチリタコス味)

Don Tacos Beef Chili Tacos by Koikeya is triangle-shaped corn chips with spicy beef chili taco flavor. Japan's take on Mexican-style snacking, it's a great snack for those who love spicy foods. It's perfect for enjoying with a cold beer or a glass of wine.

Texture: Crunchy Flavors: Beef, Chili, Spicy Best way to enjoy: Straight from the bag Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Pirikare Curry Snack (ピリカレ)

Pirikare Curry Snack by Tengu Seika is a spicy curry-flavored puffed snack from a Kyoto maker. Authentic curry spice with a kick of heat makes it a great snack for those who love spicy foods. It's perfect for enjoying with a cup of coffee or tea.

Texture: Crunchy Flavors: Curry, Spicy Best way to enjoy: Straight from the bag Where to find: Convenience stores and supermarkets

Side-by-Side Comparison

Here's a quick comparison of the snacks I've mentioned:

  • Price: 100-300 yen
  • Sweetness level: 1-5 (1 being the least sweet)
  • Texture: Crunchy, Crispy
  • Best for: Gifting, Solo, Sharing
  • Availability outside Japan: Yes, available at specialty stores and online

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Here are a few common mistakes to avoid when buying Japanese snacks:

  • Not checking the expiration date before buying.
  • Not knowing the correct pronunciation of the snack name.
  • Not trying the snack before buying a large quantity.

Pro Tips from Japan

Here are a few insider tips from Japan:

  • Try snacks that are seasonal for the best flavor.
  • Konbini (convenience stores) prices are often cheaper than drugstore prices.
