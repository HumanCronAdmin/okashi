rice crackers are a whole snack category you’re missing

if you’ve only ever eaten potato chips and cookies, you’re missing an entire world. rice crackers. crunchy things made from rice instead of wheat or potato. in japan they’re everywhere, and kameda seika is the company that makes the ones you’ll get addicted to. two products in particular: happy turn, which has a mystery powder that nobody can explain, and kaki no tane, which is the reason beer tastes better in japan.

the lineup

happy turn (ハッピーターン)

this one is pure, unadulterated comfort. the happy turn rice crackers are super light and airy, almost like a puffy chip. but the magic is that ‘happy powder’. it’s this sweet, salty, umami coating that just sticks to everything. it’s not overwhelmingly sweet, and the saltiness really makes you want another one. it’s the kind of snack you can mindlessly munch on while you’re watching something on your phone at night.

the texture is key here. it’s a gentle crunch, not too hard, and it dissolves a bit in your mouth. it’s perfect for those moments when you need a little pick-me-up but don’t want anything too intense. it’s like a warm hug in snack form, if that makes sense.

kaki no tane (柿の種)

okay, kaki no tane is a classic for a reason. these are the crescent-shaped rice crackers that are usually mixed with peanuts. they’ve got a good crunch, a definite kick of spice, and that salty, savory flavor. they’re basically the ultimate beer or sake companion. i always have a bag stashed away for when friends come over.

the mix of the crispy cracker and the crunchy peanut is what makes it. the spice isn’t insane, but it’s enough to keep things interesting. i find myself eating these most often when i’m out with friends, maybe at an izakaya or just hanging out. it’s a snack that encourages sharing and conversation.

quick comparison

snack texture sweetness (1-5) best moment konbini price
happy turn light, airy crunch 3 late-night solo snacking ¥150-200
kaki no tane crispy, crunchy 1 sharing with friends, with drinks ¥120-180

which one first?

if you’re looking for something comforting and subtly sweet, go for happy turn. if you want a bit of spice and a satisfying crunch that pairs well with drinks, grab kaki no tane.

where to find them

you can find both happy turn and kaki no tane pretty much everywhere. konbini, supermarkets, drugstores, and even on amazon japan. they’re staples.
