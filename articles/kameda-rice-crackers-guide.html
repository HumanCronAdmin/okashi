kameda kaki no tane happy turn rice crackers: what's the deal?

so my overseas friends keep asking me about konbini snacks, and it gets repetitive. like, "what should i buy?" and i'm like, "okay, well, there's pocky, then there's kitkat matcha, and then..." you get it. there's a whole world beyond that, and i got tired of repeating myself. so i started this database. today, we're talking kameda. they make a lot of the stuff i eat daily.

the lineup

happy turn (ハッピーターン)

this one is wild. it's a rice cracker, but it's coated in this powdery stuff they call "happy powder." it's super light and crispy, and the powder is this weirdly addictive sweet and salty thing. seriously, it hits your tongue and you just want more. it’s not spicy at all, just this savory, slightly sweet dust. i usually grab these when i need a little pick-me-up in the afternoon.

the texture is really delicate, like it might crumble if you look at it too hard. it’s not a loud crunch like some other crackers. the happy powder sticks to everything, so be warned, your fingers will be dusty. it’s a classic for a reason, though. it’s just… happy. lol.

kaki no tane (柿の種)

okay, kaki no tane. this is the OG beer snack. it's crescent-shaped rice crackers mixed with peanuts. the crackers themselves are crunchy, not delicate like happy turn. they have a nice salty kick, and a little bit of spice that sneaks up on you. the peanuts are a good addition, adding a different kind of crunch and saltiness.

these are pretty much everywhere. you can find them in the little snack aisles in supermarkets or even in the bigger konbini. they come in all sorts of sizes, from tiny bags to bigger ones. i usually have a bag of these when i'm watching something at night. the spice is just enough to keep things interesting.

quick comparison

snack texture sweetness (1-5) best moment konbini price (approx)
happy turn light, crispy, powdery 3 afternoon slump ¥150
kaki no tane crunchy, nutty 1 evening chill ¥120

which one first?

if you want something super light and a little sweet-salty, go for happy turn. if you prefer a more substantial crunch with a bit of spice and peanuts, kaki no tane is your guy. they’re both good, just different vibes.

where to find them

you can find both kameda's happy turn and kaki no tane at pretty much any konbini or supermarket across japan. they're also easily available on amazon if you're ordering from overseas.
