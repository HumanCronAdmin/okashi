rice crackers are a whole snack category you’re missing

if you’ve only ever eaten potato chips and cookies, you’re missing an entire world. rice crackers. crunchy things made from rice instead of wheat or potato. in japan they’re everywhere, and kameda seika is the company that makes the ones you’ll get addicted to. two products in particular: happy turn, which has a mystery powder that nobody can explain, and kaki no tane, which is the reason beer tastes better in japan.

the lineup

happy turn (ハッピーターン)

pick one up and it’s so light it barely weighs anything. bite it and the crunch is gentle, almost delicate. then the powder hits — sweet, salty, umami, all at once. kameda calls it "happy powder" and that’s the actual official name. nobody knows the recipe. it sticks to your fingers and you lick them without thinking about it.

i eat these late at night when the apartment is quiet. my downstairs neighbor brought me a bag when i was sick once and it felt like the nicest thing anyone had done all week. that’s the kind of snack happy turn is.

kaki no tane (柿の種)

crescent-shaped spicy rice crackers mixed with peanuts. pour some into your palm, toss them in your mouth, reach for your beer. that’s the loop. it’s been japan’s default beer snack since 1966. the spice builds slowly — first bite is just salty, by the fifth bite there’s a little heat. the peanuts are there to cool things down.

kameda ran a national vote in 2020 to decide the cracker-to-peanut ratio. it went from 6:4 to 7:3. people had strong opinions. i keep a bag in my kitchen for when friends come over — open it, put it on the table, and the conversation just starts. some people eat the peanuts first, some eat them last, some pick them out entirely. everyone has a system.

quick comparison

snack texture sweetness (1-5) best moment konbini price
happy turn light, airy crunch 3 late-night solo snacking ¥150-200
kaki no tane crispy, crunchy 1 sharing with friends, with drinks ¥120-180

which one first?

if you’re looking for something comforting and subtly sweet, go for happy turn. if you want a bit of spice and a satisfying crunch that pairs well with drinks, grab kaki no tane.

where to find them

you can find both happy turn and kaki no tane pretty much everywhere. konbini, supermarkets, drugstores, and even on amazon japan. they’re staples.
