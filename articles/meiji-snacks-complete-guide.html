the great mushroom vs. bamboo shoot debate

there’s a war in japan that’s been going on for decades and nobody outside the country knows about it. kinoko no yama vs takenoko no sato. mushrooms vs bamboo shoots. same company makes both. meiji has even run official popularity votes over it. people have strong opinions.

that’s meiji for you. they don’t just make chocolate — they make the kind of stuff that people argue about at dinner tables. the milk chocolate bar that’s been the same since 1926. the little rocket-shaped strawberry things your kids steal from your bag. it’s all meiji, and it’s all in every konbini within walking distance of wherever you are right now.

the lineup

takenoko no sato (たけのこの里)

the little bamboo shoot guys. bite into one and the cookie part snaps clean — it’s a shortbread-type crunch, buttery, and the chocolate on top just slides off onto your tongue. not too sweet. my roommate and i split a bag most weeknights while we’re watching something. the bag never lasts the whole episode.

fun fact: meiji has run official popularity votes between these and kinoko no yama. people actually vote. it’s a whole thing. same company makes both, and they still let their own products fight each other. that’s pretty fun.

kinoko no yama (きのこの山)

the mushroom-shaped ones. you snap the chocolate cap off the cracker stem — that’s the correct way to eat it, don’t let anyone tell you otherwise. the cracker is thinner than takenoko, lighter. the chocolate-to-cracker ratio feels different too, more chocolate per bite. i keep a box in my desk drawer and grab a couple when i need something between meetings.

the rivalry between these and takenoko is real. people at my office have opinions. someone once brought both to a team lunch and made everyone vote. kinoko won that day. barely.

meiji milk chocolate (明治ミルクチョコレート)

break off a square. let it sit on your tongue for a second. it melts slow, creamy, not too sweet. that’s it. that’s the whole experience. this bar has been the same since 1926 and nobody wants it to change. i keep one in my bag for rainy afternoons — there’s something about the sound of rain and a square of this chocolate that just works.

my neighbor gave me one of these when i first moved in. didn’t even say much, just handed it over with a cup of tea. felt like a proper welcome. it’s that kind of chocolate.

meiji apollo strawberry chocolate (明治 アポロ)

tiny pink-and-brown cones. the shape looks like it was designed for space, not for snacking — there's a story behind it. pour a few into your hand and they look like a little launch pad. the strawberry top is tart, the chocolate bottom is creamy, and you eat about five before you register that you’ve started. my friend’s kid calls these "rocket candy" and won’t share them with anyone.

kinoko no yama strawberry (きのこの山 いちご)

seasonal. pink mushrooms on the shelf in spring. the strawberry chocolate is sharper than apollo’s — less creamy, more fruit-forward. i bought a box on a whim last march and ended up going back for two more. good for when you’re walking through the park and want something that feels like the season.

which one first?

if you like a classic, go for the meiji milk chocolate bar. for a fun, fruity twist, try the apollo or the kinoko no yama strawberry. if you can’t decide between the mushroom and bamboo shoot debate, just get both, lol.

where to find them

you can find most of these at any konbini, supermarket, or even drugstores across japan. they’re also usually available on amazon japan if you’re looking to stock up.
