the great mushroom vs. bamboo shoot debate

there’s a war in japan that’s been going on for decades and nobody outside the country knows about it. kinoko no yama vs takenoko no sato. mushrooms vs bamboo shoots. same company makes both. meiji ran an actual national election over it. people have strong opinions.

that’s meiji for you. they don’t just make chocolate — they make the kind of stuff that people argue about at dinner tables. the milk chocolate bar that’s been the same since 1926. the little rocket-shaped strawberry things your kids steal from your bag. it’s all meiji, and it’s all in every konbini within walking distance of wherever you are right now.

the lineup

takenoko no sato (たけのこの里)

these are the little bamboo shoot guys. the cookie part is like a shortbread, really buttery and it snaps when you bite it. the chocolate on top is smooth, not too sweet, and it just melts in your mouth. i like these when i want something a bit more substantial than just a thin wafer. they’re perfect for when you’re just chilling at home, maybe watching something on your phone at night.

the best part is the satisfying crunch, and then that smooth chocolate finish. it feels a little more grown-up than some of the other kids' snacks, you know? i usually find a bag of these at the supermarket, and they’re pretty affordable.

kinoko no yama (きのこの山)

alright, the mushroom ones. these are the ogs, the ones everyone argues about. the cracker part is thinner than takenoko, a bit lighter. the chocolate is the same good meiji milk chocolate. when you bite into these, it’s a quicker crunch, and the chocolate hits your tongue first. i love these when i’m on the go, like walking to the station or grabbing one before a meeting.

the texture difference is subtle but it’s there. i tend to grab these when i want something a bit lighter, something that doesn’t feel like a whole production. they're also super easy to find everywhere.

meiji milk chocolate (明治ミルクチョコレート)

this is the bar. the one that’s been around forever. it’s just… perfect milk chocolate. it’s creamy, it melts super smooth, and it’s not overly sweet. it’s the kind of thing you can break off a square or two and just let it dissolve. i eat this when i need a pure hit of chocolate comfort, like after a really long day, or when it’s raining outside and i’m on the balcony.

it’s so simple but so good. you can find these everywhere, from the tiniest konbini to the biggest supermarket. it’s the baseline for good japanese chocolate, i swear.

meiji apollo strawberry chocolate (明治 アポロ)

these little cone-shaped guys are so cute. they have a pink strawberry chocolate on top and then the brown milk chocolate at the bottom. the strawberry flavor is sweet and a little tart, and it mixes really well with the creamy milk chocolate. they’re small, so you can pop a few in your mouth at once. i like these when i’m sharing with friends, or when i want something a bit fruity.

they’re a bit more fun than a plain chocolate bar. i usually see these in the snack aisle at the supermarket, and they come in little boxes.

kinoko no yama strawberry (きのこの山 いちご)

okay, so this is the strawberry version of the mushroom guys. it’s the same crispy cracker base, but instead of milk chocolate, it’s got this pink strawberry chocolate. it’s a seasonal thing sometimes, but when you find it, it’s worth grabbing. the strawberry flavor is bright and sweet, and it’s a nice change from the usual. i’ll grab these when i’m feeling a bit adventurous, maybe on a weekend afternoon.

it’s like a little burst of fruity sweetness with that familiar crunch. a fun twist on a classic.

which one first?

if you like a classic, go for the meiji milk chocolate bar. for a fun, fruity twist, try the apollo or the kinoko no yama strawberry. if you can’t decide between the mushroom and bamboo shoot debate, just get both, lol.

where to find them

you can find most of these at any konbini, supermarket, or even drugstores across japan. they’re also usually available on amazon japan if you’re looking to stock up.
