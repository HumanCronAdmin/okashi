beyond pocky and kitkat matcha

i get asked all the time what japanese snacks are actually good, like beyond the usual suspects everyone knows. and yeah, pocky is fine, kitkat matcha is a classic, but there's a whole universe out there. i live here, i eat these things every single day, and i got tired of repeating myself to my friends overseas. so i started this whole database thing to keep track of everything i’ve tried. meiji makes a ton of stuff, and some of it is seriously underrated.

the lineup

takenoko no sato (たけのこの里)

these are like little chocolate-dipped bamboo shoots. the cookie part is pretty standard, not too sweet, and has a nice crisp snap. the chocolate is smooth, not super thick, so you get a good balance. they’re perfect for when you’re sitting at your desk around 3pm and need something small. they’re not gonna blow your mind, but they’re reliable.

the biggest debate in japan is these versus kinoko no yama. i’m team takenoko, but i’ll admit it’s mostly for the shape. the taste is pretty similar.

kinoko no yama (きのこの山)

okay, the mushroom-shaped ones. these are the other half of that legendary snack rivalry. the cracker part is the same crisp cookie as takenoko, but the chocolate is on top of the "stem." i find the chocolate ratio here feels a little more concentrated on each bite.

they’re easy to pop in your mouth while you’re waiting for a train or just zoning out watching something. the crunch is satisfying.

meiji milk chocolate (明治ミルクチョコレート)

this is the OG. like, since 1926. it’s just a straightforward milk chocolate bar. the squares are thin, which means they melt pretty quickly in your mouth. it’s creamy, not too sweet, and has that classic, comforting chocolate flavor.

i usually grab a bar when i’m at the supermarket and just want something simple to have after dinner. it’s not fancy, but it’s good.

meiji apollo strawberry chocolate (明治 アポロ)

these are cute little cone-shaped candies. they have a pink strawberry-flavored chocolate on top and then a brown milk chocolate on the bottom. the strawberry part is pretty artificial tasting, but in a good, nostalgic way. it’s not overpowering.

they’re small, so you can eat a bunch without feeling too guilty. good for a quick sweet fix when you’re on the go.

kinoko no yama strawberry (きのこの山 いちご)

this is a seasonal version, usually around spring. it’s the same mushroom shape, but the chocolate is strawberry flavored. it’s a bit more vibrant pink than apollo. the strawberry flavor here is a bit sharper, less creamy than the regular milk chocolate.

if you like strawberry candy, you’ll probably enjoy these. they’re a nice change of pace from the usual chocolate.

quick comparison

apollo strawberry melts, distinct layers 3 quick sweet fix ¥130
kinoko no yama strawberry crispy cookie, strawberry chocolate 4 when you want something fruity ¥180 (seasonal)

which one first?

if you want the classic debate experience, go for takenoko no sato or kinoko no yama. if you’re a fan of simple, smooth milk chocolate, the meiji milk chocolate bar is where it’s at. and for a fruity twist, apollo or the strawberry kinoko no yama are good choices.

where to find them

you can find all of these at pretty much any konbini or supermarket across japan. they’re also usually available on amazon if you’re shopping from overseas.
