hi-chew, but make it japanese candy flavors

so my friends overseas always ask me what japanese candy they should try. and of course, the same few things come up: kitkat matcha, pocky. but there's a whole world beyond that, and i got tired of repeating myself. so i started this database, just buying and eating everything i could find. today, we're talking about hi-chew. it's everywhere, but the flavors here in japan? totally different.

the lineup

hi-chew grape (ハイチュウ グレープ)

this is the one you probably know. it's super soft, like a really intense gummy. the grape flavor is strong, almost like grape medicine but in a good way? it’s sweet, obviously, but has that classic hi-chew chewiness that lasts. i usually grab these when i need a quick sugar hit at my desk around 3pm. they're pretty reliable.

the texture is the main thing here. it's not hard like a jawbreaker, and it doesn't melt in your mouth like chocolate. it's just... chewy. the flavor is pretty straightforward grape, nothing too complicated. if you've had it before, this is pretty much it.

hi-chew strawberry (ハイチュウ ストロベリー)

this one is a bit softer, i think, than the grape. it tastes like real strawberries, not artificial at all. it’s sweet but with a little bit of tartness that cuts through the sugar. it’s one of those candies that makes you feel like you’re actually eating fruit. i like these when i’m waiting for the train, just popping one in my mouth.

the chew is still there, but it feels a little less dense than the grape. the strawberry flavor is really bright and pleasant. it’s a crowd-pleaser for sure. if you’re not sure what to pick, strawberry is always a safe bet. it’s just a good, solid fruit candy.

quick comparison

which one first?

if you like a really strong, classic grape flavor and a satisfying chew, go for the grape. if you prefer something a bit more subtle and fruity, the strawberry is the way to go. both are solid choices.

where to find them

you can find these everywhere. konbini, supermarkets, even drugstores. amazon japan has them too, of course. they're super easy to get your hands on.
