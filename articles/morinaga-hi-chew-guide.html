the candy that america doesn’t know is japanese

hi-chew is in gas stations across the US. it’s in MLB dugouts. it’s at movie theater checkout lines in like 30 countries. and most people eating it have zero idea it’s from japan. morinaga made something so good it just… blended in everywhere it went. no "exotic japanese import" label needed. it just showed up on shelves and people started buying it.

the thing is, the two flavors that made it overseas — grape and strawberry — are just the surface. in japan there are dozens of hi-chew flavors rotating through konbini shelves all year. but even just these two are worth talking about because they hit different.

the lineup

hi-chew grape (ハイチュウ グレープ)

this is the og, right? the one everyone knows. it’s super soft and chewy, almost like a gummy but a bit firmer. the grape flavor is really intense, like you’re eating actual fresh grapes, but sweeter, obviously. it’s got that classic hi-chew texture where it kind of melts in your mouth as you chew. i love eating these while i’m watching something on my phone at night, just popping them in one after another.

sometimes i’ll buy a big bag of the individual wrapped ones and just leave them in a bowl on my coffee table. it’s funny how quickly they disappear. the sweetness level is pretty high, but it’s balanced by that strong fruit flavor. it’s not overly artificial tasting, which is a big plus for me.

hi-chew strawberry (ハイチュウ ストロベリー)

the strawberry hi-chew is just as classic. it’s got that sweet, familiar strawberry taste that’s always a crowd-pleaser. like the grape, it’s super chewy and soft. i find the strawberry flavor to be a little more subtle than the grape, but still really satisfying. it’s the kind of candy you can share with anyone, and they’ll probably like it.

i remember one time i was on the shinkansen and split a pack of these with a friend. it was the perfect travel snack. the sweetness is there, but it’s a nice, fruity sweetness. it doesn’t leave that weird coating in your mouth like some candies do. it’s just pure, simple, strawberry goodness.

quick comparison

snack texture sweetness (1-5) best moment konbini price
hi-chew grape soft, juicy chewy 4 3pm slump at desk ~120 yen
hi-chew strawberry soft, juicy chewy 3.5 shinkansen ride ~120 yen

which one first?

if you want that really bold, almost grape-juice-concentrate flavor, go for the grape. if you’re in the mood for something a bit more mellow and universally loved, the strawberry is your best bet. both are great for a quick sweet fix.

where to find them

you can find hi-chew pretty much everywhere. konbini, supermarkets, drugstores, even some convenience stores in tourist areas. they’re also readily available on amazon japan if you want to stock up.
