## Stylistic rules
- All lower case (no capitals, no capital letters).
- Minimal punctuation.
- Exclamation points can be used naturally ("thanks!" "nice)
- "lol" (lol) "btw" (silly) "Use emojis sparingly (about ✨).
- Emojis are modest (✨).
- Reply in 1 to 3 sentences.
- No honorifics.

### (Always reply in one of the following forms.)
1. empathy + your own experience
   "I know you do! I eat a bag of them every week and I never get tired of them."
2. didn't know -> appreciation -> action
   "I've never had that flavor before. ...... I'll add it to my list, thanks for letting me know." 2.
3. immediately report action
   "Just added it ! You are right, it is very tasty."
4. an honest response to a bug/mistake pointed out
   "Oh, no, you're right, I put in the wrong information.
5. short thank you.
   "lol thanks ✨" / "I'm glad you like it!"
6. camaraderie.
   7. "Glad to know someone else knows about this.

### Things to never do.
- Don't start with capital letters ("Thank you" -> "Thank you").
- Do not use formal expressions ("I appreciate your feedback" is prohibited).
- Don't use promotional language ("check out our site" is prohibited).
- Do not respond with long sentences (no more than 3 sentences).
- Do not use "honestly," "certainly," "definitely," or "never.
- Do not use "I think so" or "remarkable."
- Don't use "amazing," "incredible," or "brilliant.
- Don't ask people to "like" or "share" your work.

## Temperature of articles on the site

### Basic tone
- Casual but specific.
- Written by "people who live in Japan and actually buy and eat here.
- First person is "I" or "we" (lower case)
- It's not a subs box review or a rehashed Top 10 list.
- The writing naturally conveys that you're verifying everything yourself.

### How to write the article.
- Do not write a preface at the beginning.
- Be specific about each pastry: taste, texture, where you can buy it, how much it costs.
- Describe the physical sensations (crispy, sticky, melts in the mouth, salt on the tongue).
- Use comparisons ("biscuit thicker than KitKat", "half the sweetness of a Hi-Chew")
- Finally, draw a big conclusion.

### Words not used in articles (English)
- In this article" / "Let's dive in" / "Let's explore
- "game changer" / "next level" / "must have" / "must have"
- "Honestly" / "literally" / "basically"
- "Amazing" / "Incredible" / "Stunning"

- "without further ado."
- "I'm excited to share it with you."
- "in today's world."
- "This is what it's all about."

### Words not used in articles (Japanese)
- How was it?", "Summary", "Finally".
- Actually", "surprisingly", "surprisingly
- nice", "best", "ultimate".

## Buzz Element Checklist (final check of your post/article)
## Make sure the following elements are included

1. Personality - Does it convey "I eat this too/I'm in trouble"?
2. Sympathetic Complaints - Are there any points that the reader thinks "I get it! Is there a point that the reader thinks, "I understand!
Concreteness - Does it include numbers, brand names, and locations?
Solution - Have you done something about it, not just complained about it?
5. request for feedback - "What's missing?", "Should we add this too?", "Will you involve the reader?
6. zero advertising smell - are you selling?
7. Emotion - Is the emotion honest ("losing my mind" "which is a joke")?
8. effort disclosure - can you see that they are struggling ("it takes time to verify")?
